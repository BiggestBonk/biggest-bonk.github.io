Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Elaborate on how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.
One situation in which I found myself weighing up the ethical consequences of my actions was when I was recently writing an essay to just do a bit of journalism (of sorts, not published anywhere as of yet) surrounding the games industry. Whilst I was writing, at first I didn’t strictly adhere to writing down a factual record of certain events that were brought up as supplementary evidence for my arguments. When it came time to write the real thing, I sat and thought about whether I care about small factual inaccuracies. In the end, I decided that were I in the reader’s shoes or anyone who was involved in any of the mentioned events, it’d be pretty insulting to see details omitted or recorded incorrectly, even if it’s not being published by a larger entity. I decided to research after that thought, and on reflection it was not only the right move from an ethical standpoint, but also actually improved the quality and depth of the piece itself.
How has your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity?
Throughout my life, the people I have been around and influenced the most by are those who put showing compassion towards others high in their list of priorities, along with trying to keep any harm to a minimum. As a result of this, I believe my sense of morals has been molded very strongly towards treating people fairly. In my time I have also seen plenty of examples of manipulation and exploitation, so whenever I see treatment that seems unfair or underhanded I’m generally quick to call it out, even though I’m generally fairly reserved in social situations.
Identify your strengths. How will they support you during your learning journey?
of my core strengths is my attention to detail. From a young age I have enjoyed analyzing how almost everything in life is composed of smaller, intricate parts that all play a role in its existence or function. I think this will come in handy during my learning journey as being meticulously minded should help with getting even the more granular things in order before moving on. Another one of my strengths is open-mindedness and a desire to be as flexible as possible. I enjoy hearing a range of perspectives on a given issue or task, regardless of if they align with my own. I’m a firm believer that compromise is a key part of life, given that it's part of human nature to not see eye to eye on every issue. I think this will help along my learning journey because discussion and (eventual) agreement are a key part of working in a team, and I believe this openness should lead to less friction if I find myself suggesting an approach that doesn’t gel well with the rest of my collaborators.
Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?
One limitation on my learning that I’ve recognised is that I struggle to really make myself known. I’ve always been a pretty shy person in social situations, preferring to just try to get through things without causing a fuss. This may affect my learning journey by hampering my ability to ask for help or even just present my work with a sense of pride, because I’d rather just get it over and done with so the spotlight is off of me.
Another limitation of mine is being overly anxious. I am a self-described chronic overthinker, tending to find myself in thought spirals a lot, sometimes before even actually getting to the thing I’m already so caught up on. This anxiety/stress has negatively impacted my learning journey in the past, and I think it will probably come back up given time, but I hope that I can better navigate my way through it with the lessons and methods I’ve learned since then.
Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.
one point whilst volunteering at an SPCA Op Shop, two other volunteers were butting heads about their beliefs surrounding whether dogs should only be allowed in the store with a leash/harness on. I approached the problem by trying to clarify the potential risks of allowing an off leash dog into the store, and how it could lead to the harm of another customer. This strategy was fairly effective , as the person in favour of off-leash dogs hadn’t really thought through the risks to children or other vulnerable people. They did concede on the issue after that clarification, but in retrospect I definitely could have been more understanding of their perspective as well, so they felt less like I came over to undermine them.
