What is Emotional Intelligence?
Having a grasp on how to perceive, control and utilize emotions to relate and communicate to others effectively. It can range from consciously hearing out people's grievances, having a general sense of social curiosity, to the ability to recognize when you’re feeling a strong emotion and accepting that it’s temporary.
How is EQ different from IQ?
EQ is all about your ability to identify and manage/express your emotions. It plays a vital part in helping you relate to others and communicate to others effectively. EQ is instrumental in not losing your cool and maintaining professional relationships with a calm demeanor. Whereas IQ is all about your capacity to learn, comprehend and analyze things from a more logical lens. While EQ is all about maintaining a balanced state of mind, IQ is moreso about utilizing said state of mind to work efficiently and improve the more hand-on aspects of your cognition eg. problem solving and other tests of your practical ability.
Why is EQ important?
EQ is a vital part of being a human in most landscapes, as effectively understanding other people is something that is practically unavoidable within society. Without EQ, the world would be a very different place, full of exclusively self-servient, uncaring (for lack of a better word) bastards. Because of emotional intelligence though, people have the capacity to forgive, empathize and alter their behaviour so that things are more harmonious
